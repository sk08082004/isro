The workhorse of a billion indian dreams. sun, moon, mars and beyond.

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has come a long way from launching its first rocket in 1963 to successfully landing on the Moon's south pole in 2023. With nearly 100 launch missions under its belt, ISRO continues to push the boundaries of space exploration, setting its sights on a crewed lunar mission by 2040 and an Indian space station by 2035. As the national space agency of India, ISRO's purpose is the pursuit of all space-based applications such as research, reconnaissance, and communications. It undertakes the design and development of space rockets and satellites, and undertakes explores upper atmosphere and deep space exploration missions. ISRO has also incubated technologies in India's private space sector, boosting its growth.
